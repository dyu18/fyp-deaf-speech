Demo Set 1: Integration of Deaf Speech ASR with TTS


TTS models are used to synthesise fluent speech corresponding to transcriptions produced by a Whisper model fine-tuned on deaf speech.
The TTS outputs are generated using HierSpeech++ and Coqui-TTS, which are capable of partially preserving each speaker's own voice without the deaf speech characteristics.

HierSpeech++ parameters: ttv_temperature=0.333, vc_temperature=0.333, duratuion_temperature=1, duratuion_length=0.6, denoise_ratio=0, random_seed=1111
Coqui-TTS parameters: default

Sample 1.1: Subject 23, Passage DC4
Groundtruth:
In Colonial America, wealthy families hired tutors and governesses to teach their children. Sometimes, they sent their sons to private schools.
Transcription (by fine-tuned Whisper):
In colonial America: wealthy farmers held tutors and governesses to teach their children. Sometimes they sent their sons to private schools.

Demo Set 2: Data Augmentation for Deaf Speech with Zero-Shot Voice-Cloning


A state-of-the-art zero-shot voice cloning model, Llasa, which is pre-trained on normal (typical) speech, is used to synthesise additional deaf speech samples for data augmentation, with original deaf speech samples used as prompt voices.
For a comparison of output quality, synthetic deaf speech samples generated by two Llasa models of different sizes (3B and 8B) are both presented. In general, the output quality of Llasa-8B is found to be more stable.

Llasa parameters: top_p=0.95, temperature=0.9
